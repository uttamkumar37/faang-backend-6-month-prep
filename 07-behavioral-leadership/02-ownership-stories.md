# Ownership Stories

## Purpose
Prepare stories that show you own outcomes, not only implementation tasks.

## Study Steps
- Draft the story in STAR format with concrete scope and metrics.
- Compress it into a 90-second answer and a 3-minute deep-dive version.
- Practice two follow-ups: what you would change and what signal proves impact.

## Senior Ownership Signals
- You identified an unowned risk before it became a roadmap issue.
- You aligned product, QA, SRE, and dependent teams.
- You made a reversible trade-off explicit instead of hiding it.
- You created durable follow-up: dashboard, runbook, test, or design doc.

## Story Template
- Situation: What customer or platform risk existed?
- Task: What ownership did you take without waiting?
- Action: How did you prioritize, align, implement, and de-risk?
- Result: What metric or business outcome improved?
- Reflection: What did the team do differently afterward?

## Interview Questions
- What was your exact role and decision authority?
- What trade-off did you make and why?
- What would you do differently now?
- How did you measure impact?

## Common Mistakes
- Using 'we' so much that your personal contribution is unclear.
- Over-explaining technical context before the behavioral point.
- No metric, customer impact, or durable learning.

## Self-Check
- [ ] The story fits in 90 seconds without rushing.
- [ ] The result has a number, risk reduction, or user impact.
- [ ] I can answer two follow-ups without inventing details.

## Practical Example
Example: Owning API p99 latency: traced slow endpoints, found connection pool saturation, added bounded timeouts, tuned pool sizing, and created latency SLO alerts.
