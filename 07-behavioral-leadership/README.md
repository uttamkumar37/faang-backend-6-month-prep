# Behavioral and Leadership Preparation

## Purpose
Prepare senior backend behavioral answers that demonstrate ownership, judgment, collaboration, and production maturity.

## Study Steps
- Read the STAR method first.
- Draft at least seven stories in story-bank.md.
- Practice one story every Sunday and after every mock.
- Map stories to company values during month 5 and month 6.

## Senior Backend Expectations
Interviewers expect an SDE-II candidate to show evidence of:
- Owning ambiguous backend problems end to end.
- Making trade-offs between delivery, reliability, security, and cost.
- Handling incidents with customer impact and prevention.
- Influencing peers through design docs, code review, and technical clarity.
- Mentoring without taking over.

## Required Story Set
| Story | Minimum bar |
|---|---|
| Ownership | You drove an outcome across teams or components |
| Conflict | You resolved a technical disagreement with data |
| Leadership | You raised engineering quality or direction |
| Incident | You diagnosed, mitigated, and prevented recurrence |
| Failure | You owned a miss and changed your process |
| Mentoring | Someone else improved because of your help |
| Ambiguity | You made progress with incomplete requirements |

## Interview Questions
- Why do you want this role now?
- What is your strongest backend ownership story?
- How do you handle disagreement with a tech lead?

## Common Mistakes
- Reciting project details without explaining your role.
- Using stories with no measurable result.
- Only preparing positive stories and no failure story.

## Self-Check
- [ ] I have seven STAR stories with metrics.
- [ ] Each story has a 90-second version.
- [ ] I can answer follow-ups about trade-offs and what I would change.

## Practical Example
Example: For a production incident answer, do not start with every log line. Start with impact, your role, mitigation, root cause, and prevention.
