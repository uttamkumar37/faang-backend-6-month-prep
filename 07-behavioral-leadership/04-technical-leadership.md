# Technical Leadership

## Purpose
Show SDE-II leadership through design direction, code quality, operational readiness, and influence without management authority.

## Study Steps
- Draft the story in STAR format with concrete scope and metrics.
- Compress it into a 90-second answer and a 3-minute deep-dive version.
- Practice two follow-ups: what you would change and what signal proves impact.

## Leadership Signals
- You reduce ambiguity for others.
- You make technical risks visible early.
- You mentor through design reviews and code review standards.
- You build mechanisms: templates, runbooks, dashboards, tests.

## Story Template
- Problem: Team was blocked or moving inconsistently.
- Leadership action: You created a design, standard, spike, or review path.
- Technical depth: Mention the hard trade-off.
- Result: Faster delivery, fewer incidents, lower latency, cleaner ownership.

## Interview Questions
- What was your exact role and decision authority?
- What trade-off did you make and why?
- What would you do differently now?
- How did you measure impact?

## Common Mistakes
- Using 'we' so much that your personal contribution is unclear.
- Over-explaining technical context before the behavioral point.
- No metric, customer impact, or durable learning.

## Self-Check
- [ ] The story fits in 90 seconds without rushing.
- [ ] The result has a number, risk reduction, or user impact.
- [ ] I can answer two follow-ups without inventing details.

## Practical Example
Example: Led migration from direct DB writes to outbox events by writing the ADR, pairing with two engineers, adding replay tests, and rolling out tenant by tenant.
